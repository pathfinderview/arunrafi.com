About

I write about knowledge work, AI, and the shape of careers in the next decade. This is where I think in public — short essays, one idea at a time, drafted most nights.

I chase ideas the way some people chase sunsets; never quite catching them, always loving the colour along the way. A few of the things I’ve built stuck; many fizzled. That gap keeps me curious. Influenced by the crisp prose of David Perell and Steph Ango, I draft each night, teaching myself to think by writing.

Books fuel the habit. Zorba the Greek reminds me that life is a tug-of-war between the thinker and the doer. I used to sketch daily; now the pencils come out once a month for a quick superhero portrait requested at bedtime by my son. The muscle still twitches.

I’m a quiet introvert who once ran a social-experience startup, a little like Airbnb but for meeting new people. When it closed, I felt a strange relief, proof that curiosity can outrun comfort.

I haven’t decided if dawn or midnight suits me better. Both feel like fresh starts.
